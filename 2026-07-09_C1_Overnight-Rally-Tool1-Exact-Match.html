Condition Analysis

Morning — skew-collapse-confirmed convergence at Asian 1SD upper / VXN −1σ

Signal stack:
  · NQ tagged Asian Open Grid's 1SD upper (29,890)
  · VXN swept its own −1σ (26.85), live-chart-confirmed exact
  · SKEW collapsed sharply (0.309 → 0.292 → 0.163), confirming stress
    genuinely unwinding, not just price coincidentally touching a level

Three independent readings — NQ's price-space ceiling, VXN's own volatility-space floor, and skew's directional-stress trend — aligned simultaneously. The reversal that followed is read as capped by Max Pain gravity and the absence of any 0DTE Call Wall overhead rather than by a specific structural ceiling.

The VXN spike earlier in the move wasn't random — it reflected dealers reacting to real order flow (puts getting bid, hedging pressure building) as price pushed into that zone. Once that panic resolves, that specific fuel is spent; price is a coordinate, fear is an event, and revisiting the same coordinate does not automatically reproduce the same event.

Second test — correctly not re-traded

At 2:36 PM, NQ retested 29,890 a second time. VXN did not reproduce its earlier reaction — it sat at 27.02, below its own baseline, trending toward its floor rather than spiking. No second entry was taken, consistent with treating a revisited price level as requiring independent confirmation, not inheriting the first test's validity.

Afternoon — SKEW spikes again, but this time it did NOT precede a reversal

Later in the session, 0DTE SKEW climbed to a fresh session high (0.340) while price traded near 29,924. On the surface this resembled the setup that preceded the morning's reversal — but the confirming ingredients were absent this time: overall σ stayed low (0.20), and VXN itself did not react, remaining flat near its lower range rather than spiking toward any upper SD level.

Price did not reverse. It continued higher, closing near 29,954.75 — decisively above the Asian 1SD upper that had rejected it twice earlier in the day. This is read as the framework's standing hierarchy holding, not breaking: GEX/price leads, VXN confirms — a SKEW move without a VXN price reaction is not sufficient confirmation on its own. This also matches Block 10's Volatility Floor mechanic directly: VXN sitting low and flat left no mechanical brake in place, and price was free to continue.

Key Rules Confirmed This Session

01

Live VXN anchor is the reliable basis for Tool 1. Once the confirmed live print was used as the anchor, all six modeled levels matched the live chart exactly.

02

A revisited price level does not inherit its first test's confirmation. The VXN reaction that validates a level is a finite, order-flow-driven event, not a property of the price coordinate itself.

03

σ and SKEW measure different things and can diverge. A session can show σ ticking up while SKEW falls, or SKEW spiking while σ stays flat — read them independently.

04

A SKEW spike without VXN price confirmation is not sufficient to call a reversal. The afternoon SKEW high was not accompanied by VXN itself moving, and price broke through the level instead — the standing hierarchy (GEX/price leads, VXN confirms) held.

05

Absence of a reinforcing GEX wall plus a flat VXN leaves no mechanical brake. With no 0DTE Call Wall above price and VXN sitting low/flat through the afternoon, nothing existed to mechanically arrest the advance — consistent with Block 10's Volatility Floor mechanic.
