04a — Methodology Note: Wall Strength ≠ Wall Presence

This session surfaced a standing principle for the framework: a Put Wall or Call Wall label is not a guaranteed stop price. Two distinct things need to be checked before treating a wall as a hard level:

1. Size — how large is the OI concentration relative to other levels on the chart? A wall can hold the "biggest" label pre-market simply because it's the largest of a thin field, not because it's genuinely deep or heavily defended. The 29,400 Put Wall in this session is a clear example: labeled Put Wall pre-market by default (no real competition), it gave way easily intraday — unlike the 29,277/29,200 stacks, which held for an extended fight before breaking.

2. Repositioning — walls are not static. As dealers rebalance through the session, OI concentrations can build, decay, or shift to different strikes. A wall identified pre-market is a snapshot at that moment, not a fixed structural floor/ceiling for the rest of the day.

Going forward: treat wall labels as relative-size, point-in-time reads rather than absolute stop levels, and check live OI before weighting a pre-market wall heavily in a trade decision.

The broader lesson from this session: the pre-market framework mapped the statistical range well (NDX IV expected move, Asian SD bands, ZG/Max Pain) — but live is where the real confirmation comes from. A wall that forms and holds with genuine size during the session (like the 29,277/29,200 stacks) carries more weight than any pre-market read, because it's being built by real, current positioning rather than a snapshot taken before the session opened. Pre-market gives the map; live wall formation is where caution and confirmation actually belong.
