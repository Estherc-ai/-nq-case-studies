05Volatility Asymmetry — Mechanical Flow Analysis
🔴Downside Volatility — Mechanical Brake · Observed June 26

When NQ declines sharply, retail participation in put options increases. Market makers, as the contra-party to those puts, short futures contracts to hedge their delta exposure. Once the downside move exhausts itself, market makers are required to buy back those futures positions to neutralize their risk. This mechanical forced-buying constitutes a structural brake on NQ declines. On June 26, VXN reaching 33.40+ signalled exhaustion of this put-buying cascade — the subsequent 406-point recovery in one candle reflects the mechanical nature of the reversal.

⚪Upside Volatility — Compounding Feedback Loop · Reference Only · Not Observed June 26

When NQ advances toward structural resistance, short sellers are compelled to buy futures to close positions, while market makers who sold call options must simultaneously purchase futures to hedge their delta. This creates a compounding feedback loop with no equivalent mechanical brake on the upside. VXN, constrained by its hard mathematical floor (~27.80–28.60), becomes statistically paralyzed and cannot signal further exhaustion while NQ continues advancing. This condition did not occur on June 26 and is included here for structural reference only.
