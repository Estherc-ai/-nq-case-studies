Condition Analysis

Pre-market OI level as a genuine forward indicator

The 29,808.63 open-interest concentration level was recorded 90 minutes before the open, while price was still trading more than 300 points below it. RTH open then printed at 29,811.25 — a 2.62-point difference. This is read as meaningful precision from a level identified in advance, not a level fitted to price after the fact. The distinction matters: this is a different data source (raw open-interest concentration from an index conversion tool) from the standing GEX-wall methodology (gamma-weighted open interest), and its predictive precision here is worth tracking as an independent confirmation layer going forward, alongside — not in place of — the existing GEX/Max Pain/VXN toolkit.

Session continuation through a scheduled catalyst

Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's first congressional testimony, scheduled for 10:00 AM, fell inside the trading window. Price showed no reversal through the session; the advance that began with the CPI print continued through and beyond the Asian Open Grid's 1SD upper (29,885.09) intraday before being rejected there by session end, with gains held into the afternoon. This is documented as the session's outcome rather than evidence about the testimony's content specifically, since the two cannot be cleanly separated from price action alone.

VXN behavior — a different profile from the prior no-brake sessions

VXN opened at its live anchor (26.09), advanced toward +3σ (26.94) around the New York AM session — consistent with a real, sizable realized price move genuinely repricing near-term volatility — then eased back toward baseline through the remainder of the visible window, rather than cascading into a sustained decline. This is a distinct pattern from the prior no-brake melt-up sessions this week, where VXN stayed persistently below anchor throughout. Here, VXN's rise and partial retracement is consistent with volatility responding to a genuine large move rather than a directional stress signal in either direction.

VXN +3σ coinciding exactly with the session's pullback low

Within the session, price pulled back intraday before resuming its advance. The point at which VXN reached +3σ (26.94) coincided exactly with the low of that pullback — price reversed upward from that point and held the reversal for the remainder of the session through the close. This is read as a live instance of the framework's ceiling-exhaustion mechanic: a VXN advance into ceiling territory marking a terminal stress point, after which the reversal did not require any GEX level to be reclaimed first — consistent with how this mechanic has behaved in prior sessions, here producing an upward reversal at a VXN ceiling extreme rather than the downward reversal at a VXN floor extreme seen earlier in the week.

No reversion to ZG at any point this session

Price did not test or approach ZG (29,290) at any point during the session — the entire session traded well above the ZG/Call Wall/Put Wall cluster established pre-market, with no reversion attempt. This is a clean, verifiable confirmation of sustained continuation for the full session, consistent with VXN never breaching its own floor (−1σ) at any point either — both tools agreeing that no downside brake condition was present throughout.

The 29,808 level — confirmed as a genuine multi-touch structural pivot

The pre-market OI concentration level (29,808.63) was tested multiple times during the New York morning session specifically — not simply matched once at the open — and held through the afternoon as well, with price unable to break back below it on later pressure. Repeated intraday respect for a level identified 90 minutes before the open, across both the morning window and the afternoon, is stronger confirmation than the single-touch open-price match alone.
