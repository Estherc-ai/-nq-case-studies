05 — Why Put Wall, Not Weekly Max Pain, Was the Realistic Target

Even though the session was extremely bearish, and options data confirmed the full picture (skew, IV expansion, wall structure), Weekly Max Pain (29,290) was not a realistic reversion target for this session. Two reasons:

  1. It's Friday. With 0DTE options expiring same-day, there is no time remaining for the standard Max Pain gravitational pull to exert itself — the mechanism depends on time for dealer hedging to pull price back toward the pain point, and Friday's 0DTE simply doesn't have that runway left.
  2. Time decay works against the reversion. As the session's 0DTE options bleed extrinsic value through the day, the dealer hedging pressure that would otherwise support a pull back toward Max Pain weakens rather than strengthens.

Put Wall (28,900) — a level with real, live OI backing it — was the more realistic and ultimately appropriate target for the reversal, rather than chasing a full reversion to Max Pain that the day's own structure didn't support.
